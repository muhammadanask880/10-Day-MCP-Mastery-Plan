## 📚 How to study Day 1 (do these in order)

### Step 1 — Read the lessons
Open [`01-lessons.md`](01-lessons.md) and read it carefully, slowly. Don't speed-read. Each sub-topic has an **analogy first** — really picture the analogy before moving to the definition.

### Step 2 — When you're stuck, ask an AI
Some concepts won't click on the first read. That's normal. Here are free ways to get unstuck:

- 💬 **Claude (free)** — https://claude.ai
- 💬 **Gemini (free)** — https://gemini.google.com
- 💬 **ChatGPT (free)** — https://chatgpt.com
- 💻 **Claude Code (paid CLI)** — best for learning while coding

**How to ask well:** copy the paragraph you don't understand into the chat, then say:
> *"I'm learning MCP. Explain this paragraph to me like I'm 10 years old, with a fresh analogy. Then give me one tiny code example."*

### Step 3 — Do the practice
Open [`02-practice.md`](02-practice.md). It takes 5–10 minutes. Don't skip it — looking at a real MCP server is when the abstract becomes real.

### Step 4 — Do the mini-project
Open [`03-mini-project.md`](03-mini-project.md). Write your own memo. This is the most valuable exercise of the day — it forces you to make MCP personal to *your* projects.

### Step 5 — Take the quiz
Open [`04-quiz.md`](04-quiz.md). Answer all 5 questions in your own words, in a text file or notebook.

### Step 6 — Self-evaluate with any AI 🎯

This is the magic step. Open any AI (Claude, Gemini, ChatGPT) and paste this prompt:

> *"I am studying a self-paced MCP course. Below is (1) the lesson content for the day, (2) the quiz questions, and (3) my own answers. Please:*
> *— Grade each answer out of 1 (so total out of 5).*
> *— Tell me clearly what I missed or got partially right.*
> *— Suggest one better way to phrase any weak answer.*
> *— End with one curiosity question to take into Day 2.*
>
> *=== LESSON CONTENT ===*
> *[paste contents of 01-lessons.md]*
>
> *=== QUIZ QUESTIONS ===*
> *[paste contents of 04-quiz.md]*
>
> *=== MY ANSWERS ===*
> *[paste your answers here]*"

That's your free, on-demand tutor. Use it every single day. ⭐
